Surveys and Interviews


The Goal

Discover what people really want to takeaway when they go on roadtrips. What are their frustrations when planning and taking these trips?

The Outcome

62 out of 72 people surveyed said that finding cool local spots and unique places was one of their favorite things to do on roadtrips. That comes second only to sightseeing.


The Problem

Real roadtrippers love to take the path less traveled by! These travelers value the memories of their trips with their friends and believe that the best memories occur in unique places. However, they often discover that these paths are hard to find.


The Solution

We believe connecting the right travelers with the right locals, for young adults on road trips, will give travelers new, unique experiences in unfamiliar places along their journey to their final destination.


The Story

Jessica constantly finds herself longing for new and different experiences on roadtrips. She wants to have memories with her friends that no one else has. She wants to see things that few people see, but she can only find places she happens to spot while on the road.

She has used other apps trying to discover new places, but she still doesn’t get that deeper connection. She realizes she needs to meet new people that can show her new places.

Jessica then finds BeLocal, recommended to her by a friend! She is skeptical at first but chooses to give it a try.

All she had to do was answer a few simple questions like, “Where do you want to go?” “What kind of roads do you want to take?” “What type of adventures do you like?” and the app did the rest!

To her surprise, Jessica realized that, not only did this app give her cool places to visit on every day of her road trip, but it also connected her with locals in the area!

Now she has an incredible app to help her plan her roadtrip, meet new people, and the app even surprises her with stopping points along the way that she can experience with her travel buddies.

Learning Through Testing

We learned that our product succeeded in ease of usability. All participants were able to complete the task given to them, and navigate through the app smoothly. However, we uncovered that some of the slides were difficult to read.


The Conclusion



In conclusion, I have found that there is a real market for this app. People who have interacted with the prototypes have found the idea to be very helpful.

It may sound obvious, but people can find what they know they want to see; however they can’t find what they want to see when they don’t know what that is.

I believe that through crowdsourcing and local guides, people can find what they want to see, even when they don’t know what they’re looking for.
